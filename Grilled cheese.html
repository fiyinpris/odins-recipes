Grilled cheese

Description

Cheese is a great source of calcium, fat, and protein. It also contains high amounts of vitamins A and B12, along with zinc, phosphorus, and riboflavin. According to U.S. Dairy, the overall nutritional profile of conventional, organic, and grass-fed dairy products is similar. Dairy products, particularly cheese fortified with vitamin D, can be a great way to consume the correct balance of vitamins and minerals. Bottom Line: Increasing your calcium and protein intake with cheese can keep your bones strong, and help prevent osteoporosis. As long as you don't have a sensitivity to lactose or dairy, eating cheese every day can be part of your healthy eating plan. In addition to the protein and calcium benefits, cheese is a fermented food and can supply a good source of probiotics for a healthy gut.

Ingredients:

  • 6 slices white bread
  • 4 tablespoons butter, divided
  • 2 slices Cheddar cheese

Steps

  1. Butter the bread on one side and place the bread butter-side down on a hot skillet.
  2. Top with cheese, then place another slice of bread on top (butter-side up).
  3. Preheat a nonstick skillet over medium heat. Place bread butter-side down in the hot skillet; add 1 slice of cheese. Butter a second slice of bread on one side and place butter-side up on top of cheese.
  4. Cook until lightly browned on one side; flip over and continue cooking until cheese is melted. Repeat with remaining 2 slices of bread, butter, and slice of cheese.
